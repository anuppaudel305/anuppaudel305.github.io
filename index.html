01 — About

A brief introduction

Agricultural economist working at the intersection of policy, finance, and risk.
I study how agricultural policy shapes the economic lives of farmers, with an emphasis on the US farm safety net, risk management, and rural finance.

I am a PhD student in Agricultural Economics at Kansas State University, where I work as a Graduate Research Assistant under Dr. Jennifer Ifft. My current research focuses on federal commodity programs, crop insurance, and the policy changes introduced by the One Big Beautiful Bill Act.

Before starting my PhD, I completed my Master of Science at Agriculture and Forestry University in Nepal, where my thesis examined the economics of honeybee production and marketing. My research has been published in the Journal of Agriculture and Food Research and other peer-reviewed outlets.

02 — Current Work

Featured research

Policy analysis of federal commodity programs and the farm safety net.
Active Research · 2025 – 2026

Farm safety net payments under the One Big Beautiful Bill Act

Working with Dr. Jennifer Ifft and collaborators at Kansas State and the University of Missouri's Rural and Farm Finance Policy Analysis Center, I contribute to projections and mapping of ARC-CO, PLC, and crop insurance net payouts for corn, soybeans, and wheat. This work informs producers, policy makers, and extension audiences across the country about how recent legislation reshapes federal support for agriculture.
